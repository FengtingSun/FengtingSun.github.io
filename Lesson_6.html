Lesson 6 Profiling tools

1. Profiling Tools

This lesson introduces research tools, which were created in the context of a research project, and commercial tools with profiling capabilities.

1). Definition of profiling tools

Whenever data are too voluminous to fit on a screen or a sheet of paper, data profiling is performed. It provides a column similarity functionality that finds columns whose value or n-gram distributions are similar. The aim of profiling tools is to allow a more powerful and integrated approach data profiling.

2). Disadvantage of tools

Profiling tools also have disadvanteges in usability, scalability, efficiency and functionality. For usability it is complex to configure and results are difficult to interpret. For scalability it is based on Main-memory which would limit the performance. And the uncertain using time could influence the efficiency of tasks. The last one functionality may be restricted to simplest tasks and individual columns or small column sets.

3. Commercial Tools

Data profiling is such an important capability for many data management tasks, there are various commercial data profiling applications. Plenty of industry leaders such as IBM, Microsoft and Oracle provides different type of product in order to meet the needs of commercial.

1). Pros and Cons of DBMS
  • Pro: Every database management system collects and maintains base statistics about the tables it manages.
  • Con: DBMS do not readily expose those metadata, the metadata are not always up-to-date and sometimes based only on samples, and their scope is usually limited to simple counts and cardinalities.
