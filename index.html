Data Profiling：an eLearning Resource

Welcome to this eLearning resource!

This page contains lessons that together constitute a survey of data profiling over relational data. The lessons are based on the paper Profiling relational data: a survey(Abedjan, 2015). When material is quoted verbatim this is indicated by block quotes. Some external materials are also cited through reference labels that are hyperlinks (see Quick Start for examples of both blockquotes and references).

The lessons also provide Self-Tests which aim to help you assess your understanding of the material covered in the lesson.

Overview

There are six lessons in this resource. There is first of an Introduction in which you learn the motivation of learning data profiling.

Lesson 1 addresses the issue of finding metadata which includes three existing challenges and its solutions.

Lesson 2 presents a classification of data profiling tasks, which is divided into single column and multiple columns profiling. The dependencies describes the relationship between this two columns.

Lesson 3 and Lesson 4 describes categories in single column and multiple column respectively in more detail.

After that Lesson 5 is the core lesson in this eLearning resource, it gives an technical description of strategies that tackle the complexity of dependency discovery tasks. The dependencies are divided into three categories: uniqueness, inclusion dependencies and functional dependencies.

Finally Lesson 6 introduces research tools, which were created in the context of a research project, and commercial tools with profiling capabilities.
